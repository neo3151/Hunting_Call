# Closed Beta Testing Guide: Outcall

To download and test the **Outcall** closed beta, follow these steps.

## 1. Join the Testing Group
Before you can download the app, you must be added to the authorized testers list in the Google Play Console.
- Ensure you have provided your Google Play email address to the developer.
- You will receive an invitation email or a link to join the testing program.

## 2. Opt-in to Testing
Once you are on the list, you need to opt-in via one of the following links:

### Web Link
[Join on the Web](https://play.google.com/apps/testing/com.neo3151.huntingcalls)

### Android Link
[Join on Android](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.neo3151.huntingcalls)

## 3. Download the App
After opting in, you can download **Outcall** directly from the Play Store:

[Download on Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.neo3151.huntingcalls)

> [!NOTE]
> It may take a few minutes for the "Download" button to appear after you opt-in. If you see "Item not found," wait a moment and refresh the page.

## 5. Troubleshooting "App not available"
If you see an "App not available" error message on the opt-in page, check the following:

- **Build Status:** Ensure your release build is not "Under Review". Google must approve the first release in a closed testing track before it becomes available to testers. Check the **Publishing overview** or **Closed testing** tab in the Play Console.
- **Tester List:** Confirm that the Google account you are signed into matches an email address added to the testers list in the Play Console.
- **Active Release:** Ensure you have actually "Started Rollout" for the build in the testing track. A build in "Draft" will not be visible to testers.
- **Country Availability:** Verify that the testing track is available in your country/region.
- **Wait Time:** It can sometimes take 2-24 hours for a new testing track/email list to propagate after you save changes in the Console.
